Pomegranate types

Find out which one suits you best!

Pomegranate of Peru

Light on the outside and dark burgundy on the inside, this large fruit is better suited for wine or juice than other pomegranate varieties. This is because the Peruvian fruit has a very distinctive and rich flavor. Furthermore, the resulting drinks will have a noble ruby ​​color. Absolutely beautiful!

Pomegranate of Azerbaijan

A brightly colored fruit with a thick skin. Its main feature is its juicy, fairly large seeds. The fruit's flavor is harmonious, with a perfect balance of sweet and sour. The Azerbaijani pomegranate is considered a symbol of this colorful country, as the favorable climate allows it to be grown there on a massive scale.

Pomegranate of Uzbekistan

It differs from the previous variety in its smaller grains. This "king" of fruits will especially appeal to those who don't like cloying sweetness. The pomegranate's flavor is tart, with a pleasant tartness. Use the fruit in various sauces and salads. We recommend making the well-known "Pomegranate Bracelet." This dish will instantly transform and enhance your holiday dinner.

Pomegranate of Morocco

The most unusual variety! Did you know there's a pink pomegranate with light, translucent seeds? It's also called the seedless fruit! Surprised? This fruit does have seeds, but they're very soft and barely noticeable. Kids will definitely love it. This exotic Moroccan pomegranate also has no sourness. It's just impeccably sweet. Try it to see for yourself!
